Our use of cookies and other tracking mechanisms

We and our third-party providers use cookies and other mechanisms to track information regarding your use of our site and our services. We may combine this information with other personal information we collect from you.

Cookies. Cookies are small text files that the websites you visit place on your computer or mobile device. Some cookies allow us to facilitate navigation on our site and our services, while others are used to allow a faster login process or to allow us to track your activities on our site and our services. Most web browsers accept cookies automatically, but if you prefer, you can edit your browser options to block them in the future. The Help portion of the toolbar in most browsers will tell you how to control cookies through that browser. Visitors to our site that disable cookies may browse certain areas of the site, but some features will not work. For more information about the types of cookies used by our site, please review our Cookies Policy.

Transparent GIFs, pixel tags and other technologies. Transparent GIFs (also known as tracking gifs, beacons, web beacons, or pixel tags) are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar to cookie functions. Contrary to cookies, which are stored on your computer’s hard drive, transparent gifs are embedded invisibly in web pages. We can use GIF to, among other things, monitor the activities of visitors and users of the site, help us manage content and compile statistics on the use of the site. We and our third-party service providers also use transparent GIFs in HTML emails sent to our customers to help us track response rates, identify when our emails are viewed and track when our emails are Forwarded

Third party analysis We use automated devices and applications such as the Sitecore analysis cookie to evaluate the use of your site. We can also use other means of analysis to evaluate our services. We use these tools to help us improve our services, performance and user experience. These entities may use cookies and other tracking technologies to execute their services. We do not share your personal information with these third parties.

Do not follow up Currently, our systems do not recognize the “do not follow-up” requirements of browsers. However, you can disable some tracking, as discussed in this section (eg, by disabling cookies).
